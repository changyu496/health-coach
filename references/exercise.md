# Exercise Science Reference

## Training Principles

### Progressive Overload
Gradually increase stress over time through:
- Volume (sets × reps)
- Intensity (weight/speed/incline)
- Density (less rest between sets)
- Frequency (more sessions/week)

Rate: increase ~2-5% per week for beginners, ~1-2% for intermediate

### Specificity (SAID Principle)
Adaptations are specific to the stimulus:
- Want fat loss → caloric deficit + resistance training + cardio
- Want strength → heavy loads, low reps
- Want endurance → sustained moderate intensity
- Want hypertrophy → moderate loads, higher volume

### Recovery
- Muscle groups need 48-72h between direct training
- Sleep 7-9h for optimal recovery
- CNS recovery matters for compound lifts
- Deload every 4-6 weeks (reduce volume 40-60%)

## Exercise Modalities for Fat Loss

### Hierarchy of Importance (for body composition)
1. **Nutrition** (70% of results)
2. **Resistance training** (preserve/build muscle during deficit)
3. **NEAT** (non-exercise activity: walking, standing, fidgeting)
4. **Cardio** (supplementary calorie burn)

### Resistance Training
- **Frequency:** 2-4×/week minimum
- **Focus:** Compound movements (squat, deadlift, row, press)
- **Rep ranges:** 6-12 reps for hypertrophy, 3-5 for strength
- **Sets:** 10-20 sets per muscle group per week
- **During deficit:** maintain intensity (weight on bar), reduce volume if needed

### Cardiovascular Training
| Type | Heart Rate Zone | Duration | Frequency | Best For |
|------|----------------|----------|-----------|----------|
| LISS (walking, easy cycling) | 50-65% HRmax | 30-60 min | Daily OK | Recovery, NEAT |
| Moderate (jogging, swimming) | 65-75% HRmax | 20-45 min | 3-5×/week | Cardiovascular health |
| Vigorous (running, HIIT) | 75-85% HRmax | 20-30 min | 2-3×/week | Fitness, time-efficient |
| Incline walking/treadmill | 60-75% HRmax | 20-45 min | Daily OK | Low-impact fat burn |

**HRmax estimation:** 220 − age (rough), or 207 − 0.7 × age (more accurate)

### NEAT Optimization
For sedentary workers, increasing NEAT often matters more than formal exercise:
- Standing desk periods
- Walking meetings
- 5-min movement breaks every hour
- Take stairs
- Park farther away
- Target 7,000-10,000 steps/day

## Injury Considerations

### Shoulder Injuries (Common)
**Avoid:**
- Overhead pressing (barbell/dumbbell)
- Behind-neck anything
- Upright rows
- Deep dips
- Heavy bench press with wide grip

**Modify:**
- Reduce bench press ROM (use floor press)
- Use neutral grip for pressing
- Lower weight, increase reps
- Avoid sleeping on injured side

**Rehab exercises:**
- Band pull-aparts (daily, 3×15)
- External rotation with band
- Face pulls (light, 3×15)
- Scapular wall slides
- YTWL raises (light dumbbells)

### Knee Issues
- Avoid deep squats if painful; use box squat to parallel
- Leg press with controlled ROM
- Straight-leg work may be fine (RDL, hamstring curl)
- Step-ups (low box)

### Lower Back
- Avoid loaded spinal flexion
- Hip hinge with neutral spine
- Core bracing (McGill Big 3: curl-up, side plank, bird dog)
- Build up deadlift gradually

## Swimming Specifics

**Calorie burn (approximate per hour, 70-80kg person):**
| Stroke | Moderate | Vigorous |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Freestyle | 400-500 | 600-700 |
| Breaststroke | 350-450 | 500-600 |
| Backstroke | 350-450 | 500-600 |
| Butterfly | 500-600 | 700-800 |

**Benefits:**
- Low impact (joint-friendly)
- Full body workout
- Good for respiratory conditions (humid environment)
- Cardiovascular + muscular endurance

**For fat loss:** 30-60 min sessions, 2-4×/week, combine with resistance training

## Heart Rate Zones

| Zone | % HRmax | RPE | Primary Benefit |
|------|---------|-----|----------------|
| 1 (Recovery) | 50-60% | Very easy | Active recovery |
| 2 (Endurance) | 60-70% | Easy conversation | Fat oxidation, base fitness |
| 3 (Tempo) | 70-80% | Uncomfortable | Aerobic capacity |
| 4 (Threshold) | 80-90% | Hard, short sentences | Lactate threshold |
| 5 (Max) | 90-100% | All-out | VO2max, anaerobic |

**Fat burning zone myth:** Zone 2 burns higher % of fat, but Zone 3-4 burns more total calories and total fat. Mix zones for best results.

## Programming Templates

### Beginner Fat Loss (3 days/week)
```
Day A: Lower Body + Core
- Goblet Squat 3×10
- Romanian Deadlift 3×10
- Walking Lunges 2×12/side
- Plank 3×30-45s

Day B: Upper Body + Core  
- Push-ups 3×max
- Cable/Band Row 3×12
- Lateral Raise 3×15
- Face Pull 3×15
- Dead Bug 3×10/side

Day C: Full Body + Cardio
- Squat variation 3×10
- Row variation 3×10
- Incline walk 20 min

+ Daily: 7000+ steps
```

### Intermediate (4 days/week, Upper/Lower split)
```
Day 1: Upper Push + Pull
Day 2: Lower Quad-dominant
Day 3: Rest
Day 4: Upper Pull + Push
Day 5: Lower Hip-dominant
Day 6-7: Active recovery / cardio
```
