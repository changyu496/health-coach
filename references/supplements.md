# Supplements Reference — Evidence-Based

## Tier 1: Strong Evidence, Widely Recommended

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Purpose | Notes |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|-------|
| Vitamin D3 | 1000-4000 IU/day | With fat-containing meal | Bone health, immunity, mood | Test blood levels; >2000 IU needs monitoring |
| Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) | 1-3g combined EPA+DHA | With meals | Inflammation, heart, brain | Choose high EPA+DHA, not just "fish oil" |
| Creatine Monohydrate | 3-5g/day | Any time, daily | Strength, muscle, cognitive | Most studied supplement; safe long-term |
| Whey Protein | 20-40g/serving | Post-workout or as needed | Meet protein target | Not necessary if diet is sufficient |
| Magnesium (glycinate/citrate) | 200-400mg | Before bed | Sleep, cramps, recovery | Avoid oxide form (poor absorption) |
| Fiber supplement (psyllium) | 5-10g/day | Before meals with water | Satiety, gut health, cholesterol | Increase gradually to avoid bloating |

## Tier 2: Moderate Evidence, Situational

| Supplement | Dose | When to Consider | Notes |
|-----------|------|------------------|-------|
| Caffeine | 100-300mg | Pre-workout (30-60 min) | Performance enhancer; tolerance builds |
| Melatonin | 0.5-3mg | 30-60 min before bed | Jet lag, sleep onset; lowest effective dose |
| Zinc | 15-30mg | With food | If deficient; excess inhibits copper |
| Iron | As prescribed | Empty stomach + Vit C | Only if blood test confirms deficiency |
| Vitamin B12 | 250-1000μg | Morning | Vegans, elderly, or if deficient |
| Probiotics | Strain-specific | Varies | Gut health; quality varies enormously |
| Collagen peptides | 10-15g/day | Any time | Joint health, skin; moderate evidence |

## Tier 3: Popular but Limited Evidence

| Supplement | Reality Check |
|-----------|---------------|
| BCAAs | Unnecessary if protein intake is adequate |
| Glutamine | No benefit for healthy individuals eating enough protein |
| CLA | Minimal fat loss effect, not worth the cost |
| Fat burners | Mostly caffeine + fillers; some ingredients dangerous |
| Testosterone boosters | OTC versions don't meaningfully raise T |
| Detox/cleanse products | Body has liver and kidneys; these are marketing |

## Interactions & Warnings

| Combination | Risk |
|-------------|------|
| Iron + Calcium | Take separately (calcium blocks iron absorption) |
| Iron + Coffee/Tea | Take iron 1h away from caffeine |
| Vitamin D + K2 | Take together (K2 directs calcium to bones) |
| Fish oil + Blood thinners | Consult doctor (additive anticoagulant effect) |
| Magnesium + Antibiotics | Space 2h apart |
| St John's Wort + Many drugs | Major interaction risk — avoid without medical advice |
| High-dose Vitamin E | May increase mortality; stay ≤400 IU |
| High-dose Vitamin A | Toxic in excess; prefer beta-carotene from food |

## Special Populations

### During Fat Loss
- **Priority:** Protein, Vitamin D, Magnesium, Omega-3
- **Consider:** Fiber supplement (satiety), Creatine (preserve muscle)
- **Avoid:** "Fat burners" (waste of money, potential harm)

### For Joint Health (injury rehab)
- Collagen peptides 10-15g + Vitamin C (before rehab exercises)
- Omega-3 (anti-inflammatory)
- Glucosamine + Chondroitin (mixed evidence, some people respond)

### For Sleep
- Magnesium glycinate 200-400mg
- Melatonin 0.5-1mg (lowest dose)
- Glycine 3g before bed
- Avoid: high-dose melatonin (>5mg counterproductive)

### For Respiratory Issues
- Vitamin D (immune modulation)
- Omega-3 (anti-inflammatory)
- NAC (N-acetyl cysteine) 600-1200mg (mucolytic, antioxidant)
- Quercetin 500-1000mg (antihistamine properties, moderate evidence)
