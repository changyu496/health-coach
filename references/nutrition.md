# Nutrition Reference — Clinical Grade

## Energy Balance

### Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)
**Mifflin-St Jeor (preferred):**
- Male: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161 + 5 = 10W + 6.25H − 5A + 5
- Female: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161

**Activity Multipliers (TDEE = BMR × factor):**
| Level | Factor | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Sedentary | 1.2 | Desk job, minimal exercise |
| Lightly Active | 1.375 | Light exercise 1-3 days/week |
| Moderately Active | 1.55 | Moderate exercise 3-5 days/week |
| Very Active | 1.725 | Hard exercise 6-7 days/week |
| Extremely Active | 1.9 | Athlete / physical labor + training |

### Safe Deficit/Surplus Ranges
- **Fat loss:** 300-750 cal/day deficit (0.25-0.75 kg/week loss)
- **Aggressive fat loss:** 750-1000 cal/day (only for obese individuals, short-term)
- **Muscle gain:** 200-400 cal/day surplus
- **Minimum intake:** Males ≥1500 cal/day, Females ≥1200 cal/day
- **Rate limit:** Do not exceed 1% body weight loss per week

### Carbohydrates
| Context | Intake (g/kg) | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Fat loss | 2-4 | Prioritize around workouts |
| Maintenance | 3-5 | Adjust to energy needs |
| Endurance athletes | 5-8 | Higher for glycogen |
| Keto | <50g total/day | Not recommended long-term for most |

**Glycemic Index considerations:**
- Low GI (<55): oats, sweet potato, most fruits, legumes — prefer these
- Medium GI (56-69): brown rice, whole wheat bread
- High GI (>70): white rice, white bread, sugar — limit, OK post-workout

**Fiber target:** 25-35g/day (14g per 1000 cal)

### Fats
| Context | Intake | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| General | 0.5-1.5 g/kg | Never below 0.5g/kg (hormones) |
| Fat loss | 0.7-1.0 g/kg | Don't cut too low |
| Minimum | 20% of total calories | Essential for hormone production |

**Fat quality priorities:**
1. Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): fatty fish 2×/week, or supplement
2. Monounsaturated: olive oil, avocado, nuts
3. Limit saturated: <10% total calories
4. Avoid trans fats: zero tolerance

| Potassium | Processed food diet | Cramps, weakness, BP issues | Banana, potato, spinach, avocado |

## Meal Timing

- **Meal frequency:** 3-5 meals/day, minimum 3 hours apart
- **Pre-workout:** Light carbs + protein 1-2h before (or fasted if tolerated)
- **Post-workout:** Protein within 2h (anabolic window is wider than once thought)
- **Pre-sleep:** Casein protein OK, avoid large meals 2h before sleep
- **Intermittent fasting:** Valid approach, not superior to regular meals for fat loss. Ensure adequate protein when eating.

## Hydration

- **Baseline:** 35ml per kg body weight
- **Exercise:** Add 500-1000ml per hour of exercise
- **Signs of dehydration:** Dark urine, headache, fatigue, reduced performance
- **Electrolytes:** Consider supplementing sodium/potassium/magnesium during heavy sweating or low-carb diets

## Portion Estimation from Photos

When analyzing meal photos:
1. Use plate/bowl as size reference (~22cm dinner plate, ~15cm rice bowl)
2. Palm = ~100g protein, fist = ~150g carbs, thumb = ~15g fat
3. Estimate portions conservatively (people tend to underestimate)
4. When uncertain, provide a range (e.g., 350-450 cal)
5. Ask user to confirm unusual items or quantities
