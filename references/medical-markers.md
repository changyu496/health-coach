## Respiratory — FeNO (Fractional Exhaled Nitric Oxide)

**FeNO50 (lower airway):**
| Level (ppb) | Interpretation |
|-------------|---------------|
| <25 | Normal — eosinophilic airway inflammation unlikely |
| 25-50 | Intermediate — interpret in clinical context |
| >50 | High — eosinophilic airway inflammation likely, suggests asthma/allergic component |

**Trend monitoring:** Reduction of >20% or >10ppb is clinically significant improvement.

**FnNO (nasal NO):**
| Level (ppb) | Interpretation |
|-------------|---------------|
| <100 | Low — consider PCD if clinically suspected |
| 100-250 | Normal |
| 250-500 | Elevated — allergic/non-allergic rhinitis |
| >500 | High — significant nasal inflammation |

**Exercise considerations with elevated FeNO:**
- Warm up gradually (reduce exercise-induced bronchoconstriction)
- Swimming in humid environments often well-tolerated
- Avoid exercising in cold/dry air or high pollution
- Use prescribed inhaler 15-30 min before exercise if applicable

## Interpreting Results — Guidelines

1. **Single values mean little** — always look at trends over time
2. **Context matters** — post-exercise, time of day, hydration, recent meals all affect results
3. **Reference ranges vary by lab** — always use the lab's own ranges
4. **Out of range ≠ disease** — mild deviations are often benign
5. **Always defer to physician** — flag concerning values and recommend follow-up
6. **Medication effects** — many medications alter lab values; document what user takes
